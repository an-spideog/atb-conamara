# 11.  Compound prepositions
## 11.1 Explanation
What's meant here, is prepositions which are followed by the genitive: *de bharr, os cionn,* etc. Because of that, prepositions like *timpeall* are included which aren't compound from an etymological perspective.

When one of these prepositions is governing a pronoun, it's normally in the possessive form: *dá bharr, os a chionn.* But the possessive form can't be put with some of them.

Only the most common compound prepositions are given here.

## 11.2 *ar feadh* /er´ f´a/
Example: *bhí mé ag obair ar feadh an lae.*  
A possessive adjective can't be added to it.

## 11.3 *ar fud*
Example: *tá siad ar fud na háite.*  
It's rare for the possessive adjective to be added to it.

## 11.4 *ar nós*
Often the *ar* is left out.  
Example: *d'imigh sé (ar) nós na gaoithe.*

It's rare for the possessive adjective to be added to it; *(ar) nós mé féin* is said instead of *ar mo nós féin*.

## 11.5 *chun* /ən/
Example: *chuir mé an litir chun bealaigh.*  
*chun* is mostly found in adverbial phrases like *chun bealaigh: chun chinn, chun deire, chun tosaigh,* etc. In some places, *ag goil chun na scoile (a' goil 'na scoile);* but *ag goil chuig an scoil* is what's said most. In Carna, *ag goil chun an Chlocháin (a' goil 'na Chlocháin)* is said.

## 11.6 *de bharr*
Examples: *bhí tuirse air de bharr an lae; sin a raibh dá bharr acu.*

## 11.7 *de réir*
The *de* is often dropped: *'réir. léir, léar* are said in some places.  
Examples:*ag obair de réir an chloig, agus mar sin dá réir.*

## 11.8 *faoi cheann*
Example: *tiocfaidh sé faoi cheann seachtaine arís.*  
The possessive adjective can't be added to it.

## 11.9 *go ceann*
Example: *ní thiocfaidh sé go ceann seachtaine eile.*  
The possessive adjective can't be added to it.

## 11.10 *i ndiaidh* /ə n´iə/
Examples: *bhí sé ag rith i ndiaidh an chairr; rith sé i mo dhiaidh.*

## 11.11 *in aghaidh* /ə nai/
Examples: *bhí mé ag siúl an aghaidh an aird; bhíodh daoine 'na n-aghaidh*

## 11.12 *le haghaidh*
/l´ai/ is usually said in Cois Fharraige, /l´ehi:/ in Carna.  
Examples: *áit mhaith le haghaidh ronnach; is len' aghaidh sin é. Cé le haghaidh é* is said (i.e. cad chuige é).

## 11.13 *os cionn (as cíonn)*
Examples: *bhí sé scríofa os cionn an doiris; le scór blianta nó as a chionn.*

## 11.14 *tar éis*
*Théis* is what's said the most, but *léis* in some places. There are other versions as well.  
Example: *bhí muid tuirseach tar éis an lae.*  
The possessive adjective can't be added to it.

## 11.15 *timpeall*
Often /h/ at the start: *thimpeall.*  
Example: *sheol sé timpeall an oileáin.*

To add a possessive adjective, the simple preposition *i* is put before it: *bhí slua 'na thimpeall*. *timpeall air* can be said too.

## 11.16 *trasna*
Example: *Shiúil sé trasna an bhóthair.*  
The possessive adjective can't be added to it except in sentences like *ghearr sé ar a thrasna é.*
